Earth & Moon as a System




As a necessary complement to the stability of the rotational axis of the planet earth is a necessary part that allows regularity in the climactic seasons, life could not develop without the existence of the moon because the seasons would be temperature outbursts among other effects.





Both the Moon's natural prominence in the earthly sky and its regular cycle of phases as seen from Earth have provided cultural references and influences for human societies and cultures since time immemorial. Such cultural influences can be found in language, lunar calendar systems, art, and mythology.





Lunar Eclipse




Occurs when the Moon passes directly behind Earth and into its shadow. During a total lunar eclipse, Earth completely blocks direct sunlight from reaching the Moon.





The only light reflected from the lunar surface has been refracted by Earth's atmosphere. This light appears reddish for the same reason that a sunset or sunrise does: the Rayleigh scattering of bluer light. Due to this reddish color, a totally eclipsed Moon is sometimes called a blood moon.
