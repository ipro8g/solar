The Biggest Planet







Jupiter can be bright enough for its reflected light to cast shadows, and is on average the third-brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon and Venus, a planet with such a large size that it can even be observed along with the Galilean moons from the earth with the naked eye.





A complete Planetary System








Surrounding Jupiter is a faint planetary ring system and a powerful magnetosphere, being explored multiple times by robotic spacecraft, 79 moons have been discovered so far including Ganymede, the largest of these, has a diameter greater than that of the planet Mercury.





In another Moon so far, there is no evidence that life exists as in Europe, but has emerged as one of the most likely locations in the Solar System for potential habitability.








A Stormy World







Jupiter is perpetually covered with clouds composed of ammonia crystals and possibly ammonium hydrosulfide, arranged into bands of different latitudes, known as tropical regions.





The interactions of these conflicting circulation patterns cause storms and turbulence and the zones have been observed and they have remained sufficiently stable for scientists to give them identifying designations.





Supporting the idea of water clouds are the flashes of lightning detected in the atmosphere of Jupiter, these electrical discharges can be up to a thousand times as powerful as lightning on Earth.





The Great Red Spot is large enough to accommodate Earth within its boundaries, Mathematical Models suggest that the storm is stable and may be a permanent feature of the planet, however, it has significantly decreased in size since its discovery. Initial observations in the late 1800s.
