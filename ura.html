A Dead World



Uranus' internal heat is lower than that of the other giant planets and it has the coldest planetary atmosphere in the Solar System.





One of the hypotheses for this suggests that when Uranus was hit by a supermassive impactor, which caused it to expel most of its primordial heat, it was left with a depleted core temperature.





Moons



Uranus has 27 known natural satellites, The five main satellites are Miranda, Ariel, Umbriel, Titania, and Oberon.





They are ice–rock conglomerates composed of roughly 50% ice and 50% rock.

Planetary Rings



The Uranian rings are composed of extremely dark particles, which vary in size from micrometres to a fraction of a metre. thirteen distinct rings are presently known, the matter in the rings may once have been part of a moon (or moons) that was shattered by high-speed impacts.





In April 2006, images of the new rings from the Keck Observatory yielded the colours of the outer rings: the outermost is blue and the other one red, in contrast, Uranus' inner rings appear grey.
