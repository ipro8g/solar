Exploration




Dozens of crewless spacecraft, including orbiters, landers, and rovers, have been sent to Mars by the Soviet Union, the United States, Europe, and India to study the planet's surface, climate, and geology.






When the Mariner 9 probe arrived at Mars in 1971, scientists saw a near planet-wide dust storm with only the giant volcano Olympus Mons showing above the haze. The storm lasted for a month, an occurrence scientists have since learned is quite common on Mars.








Martian Canals & Hydrology




By the 19th century, the resolution of telescopes reached a level sufficient for surface features to be identified, the Italian astronomer Giovanni Schiaparelli produced the first detailed map of Mars and notably contained features he called canali. Even in the 1960s articles were published on Martian biology, putting aside explanations other than life for the seasonal changes on Mars. Detailed scenarios for the metabolism and chemical cycles for a functional ecosystem have been published.






On the planet mars water in a liquid state is in very specific places and conditions.








Mars has two permanent polar ice caps, during a pole's winter, it lies in continuous darkness and when the poles are again exposed to sunlight, the frozen CO2 sublimes.

Geometric Structures



Eighteen images of the Cydonia region were taken by the orbiters, In one of the images taken by Viking 1 on July 25, 1976, had the appearance of a humanoid face, this region on the planet Mars has attracted both scientific and popular interest.








The Phobos monolith is a large rock on the surface of Mars's moon Phobos, is a boulder about 90m tall, is a geological feature consisting of a single massive piece of rock.





The Mars monolith a rectangular object discovered on the surface of Mars, is estimated to measure about 20 meters.
